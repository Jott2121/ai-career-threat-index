## Restatements

Moves larger than ±8 points require a stated reason:

- **Radiologist** (45 → 34): v2's realization ceiling caps the score at task-automation potential rather than a flat composite; imaging AI's maturity and adoption have both climbed sharply in 2026, but complex and interventional work still bounds what's automatable, pulling the score down from v1's higher composite.
- **Healthcare Administrator** (42 → 30): v2's realization ceiling (task automation as a hard cap, agentic factor weighted at only 10%) scores this role lower than v1's flatter composite; back-office administration is more agent-automatable than ever, but core management judgment still caps the ceiling well below where v1 placed it.
- **Lab Technician** (48 → 30): Under v2's realization ceiling, hands-on specimen handling caps automation well below where v1's flatter composite placed this role; instrument and QC automation are real but don't reach the physical core of the job.
- **Paralegal** (58 → 48): Methodology v2's realization model plus a role-specific reassessment of tool maturity and adoption produce a materially lower composite than the v1 weighted average; core paralegal output remains substantial, but a large share (client contact, case strategy support) is judgment-gated work AI does not yet own end-to-end.
